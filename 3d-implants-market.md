<p><p>The 3D implants market encompasses three primary types: metal, ceramic, and polyethylene. Metal implants, typically made from titanium or stainless steel, offer high strength and biocompatibility, making them ideal for load-bearing applications. Ceramic implants, known for their excellent aesthetics and biocompatibility, are favored in dental and orthopedic uses. Polyethylene implants are lightweight and flexible, commonly used in joint replacements, providing good wear resistance. Each material type serves specific applications, addressing diverse patient needs and surgical requirements.</p></p>
