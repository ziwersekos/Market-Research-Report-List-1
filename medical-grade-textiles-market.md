<p><p>The medical-grade textiles market encompasses three primary fabric types: non-woven, woven, and knitted fabrics. Non-woven fabrics are engineered for single-use applications, offering barriers against fluids and bacteria, ideal for surgical gowns and masks. Woven fabrics are durable and breathable, commonly used in devices like surgical drapes and patient garments, providing structural integrity. Knitted fabrics offer flexibility and comfort, suitable for garments requiring stretch, such as compression bandages. Each type serves distinct purposes in healthcare settings, ensuring patient safety and comfort.</p></p>
