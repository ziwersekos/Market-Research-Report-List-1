<p><p>The respiratory antivirals market consists of various types, including nucleoside analogs, neuraminidase inhibitors, ion channel blockers, and fusion protein inhibitors. Nucleoside analogs mimic natural nucleosides, disrupting viral replication. Neuraminidase inhibitors block viral enzyme action, preventing the spread of influenza. Ion channel blockers inhibit viral entry into host cells, while fusion protein inhibitors prevent virus-host cell fusion. Together, these categories provide diverse therapeutic strategies for treating viral respiratory infections, addressing public health challenges and enhancing patient outcomes.</p></p>
