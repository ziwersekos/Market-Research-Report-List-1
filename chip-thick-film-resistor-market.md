<p><p>The Chip Thick Film Resistor market is primarily segmented into two types: Through Hole and Surface Mount Device (SMD) resistors. Through Hole resistors are designed for insertion into pre-drilled boards, offering robust physical stability, making them suitable for high-power applications. Conversely, SMD resistors are compact and mounted directly onto circuit boards, providing advantages in space-saving and automated assembly processes. Both types serve diverse applications in electronics, catering to various performance requirements and manufacturing preferences in the industry.</p></p>
