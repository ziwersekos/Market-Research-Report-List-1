<p><p>The gene therapy market can be segmented into viral and non-viral delivery methods. Viral-based gene therapies utilize modified viruses to deliver genetic material into cells, ensuring efficient uptake and expression. These therapies often show higher transduction efficiency but come with potential immunogenicity concerns. In contrast, non-viral gene therapies employ methods like plasmid DNA, liposomes, or physical techniques to introduce genetic material, offering advantages in safety and scalability, though typically facing challenges in delivery efficiency and duration of expression.</p></p>
