<p><ul><li>Solid-State</li><li>Mechanical</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The LiDAR distance sensor market is primarily divided into two types: solid-state and mechanical LiDAR sensors. Solid-state LiDAR utilizes fixed-position components, offering durability and compactness, making it suitable for automotive and robotics applications. In contrast, mechanical LiDAR employs rotating parts to capture 360-degree data, providing high-resolution mapping but often at a higher cost and larger size. Both types serve industries such as autonomous vehicles, agriculture, and environmental monitoring, each with unique advantages and limitations tailored to specific needs.</p></p>
