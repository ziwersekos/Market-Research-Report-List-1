<p><p>The Battery for Railways market comprises three primary types: Lead-Acid Batteries, Li-Ion Batteries, and Ni-Cd Batteries. Lead-Acid Batteries are known for their affordability and reliability, commonly used for starting engines and auxiliary power. Li-Ion Batteries offer higher energy density, lighter weight, and longer cycle life, making them suitable for modern electric trains. Ni-Cd Batteries, though less common now, provide robust performance in extreme conditions and fast charging capabilities, often utilized in older systems and specific applications.</p></p>
