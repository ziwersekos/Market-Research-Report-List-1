<p><p>Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM) is a powerful imaging technique used to examine the surface structure and composition of samples at high resolution. The SEM market is segmented into standard scanning electron microscopes and variable-pressure scanning electron microscopes. Standard SEMs operate in a vacuum, providing high-resolution images of conductive samples, while variable-pressure SEMs allow observation of non-conductive materials without extensive sample preparation by operating at lower vacuum levels. Both types cater to diverse applications in materials science, biology, and nanotechnology.</p></p>
