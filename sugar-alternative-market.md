<p><p>The sugar alternative market consists of three main types. High fructose syrup, derived from corn, provides a sweet flavor with lower production costs but raises health concerns. High-intensity sweeteners, such as aspartame and stevia, offer intense sweetness with minimal calories, appealing to health-conscious consumers. Low-intensity sweeteners, like sugar alcohols, provide a milder sweetness and fewer calories, making them popular for certain low-sugar products. Together, these alternatives cater to diverse consumer preferences for taste and health considerations.</p></p>
