<p><p>The scintillation materials market comprises three main types: solid, liquid, and gaseous scintillators. Solid scintillators are typically made from crystalline materials and are known for their durability and efficient light yield, often used in radiation detection. Liquid scintillators are solutions that emit light when ionizing radiation passes through, offering versatile applications in nuclear physics. Gaseous scintillators utilize gas mixtures to detect radiation through ionization, providing real-time measurements in various environments. Each type serves unique applications across industries such as nuclear, medical, and security.</p></p>
