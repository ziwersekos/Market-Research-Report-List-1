<p><p>The urethral stricture market is segmented into two primary types: Anterior Urethral Stricture (AUS) and Posterior Urethral Stricture (PUS). AUS occurs in the penile urethra and is often caused by trauma or infection, affecting male patients predominantly. PUS, on the other hand, involves the prostatic and membranous urethra, typically arising from surgeries or pelvic fractures. Both stricture types require different treatment approaches, influencing market dynamics through varying patient demographics, prevalence rates, and therapeutic innovations.</p></p>
