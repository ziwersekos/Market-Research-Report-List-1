<p><p>The lab-created diamond market features various types of grains, each distinguished by their production methods and characteristics. RVD (Regular Vapor Deposition) grains are created through chemical vapor deposition, offering high quality. MBD (Microwave-based Diamond) grains utilize microwave technology, enhancing affordability. SCD (Single Crystal Diamond) grains are produced as a single crystal, ensuring exceptional clarity. SMD (Synthetic Monocrystalline Diamond) grains are similar but may include variations in processes. DMD (Diamond Mosaic Diamond) grains combine multiple structures, appealing to those seeking unique designs.</p></p>
